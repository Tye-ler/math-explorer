Trig Explorer

Move the angle on the unit circle and watch the six trig functions trace over multiple revolutions. Sine and cosine come straight from the coordinates; the others are the corresponding ratios—and they repeat as θ grows.

explanation

How to read this: the moving point is (cos θ, sin θ). Projections to the axes show cosine and sine as lengths (with sign from the quadrant). The graph uses the same θ on the horizontal axis from 0 to 2π times the revolution count. Tangent, cotangent, secant, and cosecant blow up where their defining ratio divides by zero; the graph leaves gaps instead of connecting across those jumps. Asymptote lines on the graph appear only while a related function is visible (tan or sec for cos θ = 0; cot or csc for sin θ = 0). Dragging the angle slider pauses animation.
